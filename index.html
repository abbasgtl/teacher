Introduction

Every Indian student dreams of studying at IITs, with top IIT JEE graduates aiming for MIT, Oxford, Cambridge, or Stanford. Many IIT alumni further their studies by pursuing Masters at these prestigious universities, showcasing the quality of education in the West. Direct Path to Success

Instead of the longer route of clearing IIT JEE, completing a Bachelor of Engineering, and then pursuing a Masters in the West, a more direct path is excelling in IGCSE/AS/A levels for admission in the top universities at bachelors level. With increasing wealth among Indians, there is a growing opportunity to invest in children's education for placements in the world's top universities.

Comprehensive Syllabus

This syllabus is covered if the student does not have a specific syllabus requirement

1. Integers & Number Systems

  • The ordinary process of number manipulation
  • Prime Numbers, factors, Multiples
  • Indices, Powers and Roots
  • Simple manipulation of Surds
  • Rationalising the denominator
  • Natural Numbers, Integers, rational and irrational numbers
  • Weights, Measures and Money
  • Fractions, Ratios, proportions, decimals and Percentages
  • Expressing numbers to a given degree of accuracy
  • Solve problems using upper and lower bounds where values are given to the degree of accuracy
  • Numbers in standard form

2. Sets

  • Idea of a set
  • Set language and notation
  • Union and Intersection of sets
  • Number of elements in a set
  • Complimentary set
  • Subsets, Universal set, Null set
  • Venn diagrams and their use in simple logic problems
  • Use of symbols to represent set

3. Algebra

  • The basic process of algebra
  • The construction interpretation and use of formulae and their manipulation
  • The factorization of simple algebraic expressions
  • Use of factor theorem
  • Algebraic division of a cubic by a linear factor
  • The manipulation of simple algebraic fractions (denominator: numerical, linear or quadratic)
  • Solutions to equations containing 1st, 2nd or 3rd degree containing one unknown quantity
  • Solutions to linear simultaneous equations in two unknown
  • Solve simultaneous equation in two unknowns (one linear, one quadratic)
  • Solution of linear inequalities and representation on number line and two dimensional space
  • Solve quadratic inequalities in one unknown and represent solution set on number line
  • The idea of a sequence

4. Functions

  • Function, Mapping, Notations, domain and range
  • Composite and inverse functions
  • Direct and indirect proportion
  • Rectangular cartesian coordinate system
  • Differentiation of integer powers
  • Determinant of gradients, rate of change
  • Maxima, minima and turning points
  • Recognise, plot and draw graphs with cubic equation
  • Calculate gradient of straight line given coordinates of two points
  • Find equation of straight line parallel/perpendicular to given line
  • Sin, cos, tan for angles of any size (in degrees)

5. Matrices

  • Representation of data by a matrix
  • Addition and multiplication of Matrices
  • Multiplication by scalar
  • Unit matrix and Null matrix
  • Determinants and inverse of non-singular 2x2 matrices
  • Transformation of the plane associated with 2x2 matrices
  • Combination of transformation

6. Geometry

  • Geometrical reasoning
  • Angle properties of parallel lines, triangles and polygons (including regular polygons)
  • Properties of parallelogram, rectangle, square, rhombus, trapezium and kite
  • Symmetry about a point, line or plane
  • Use Pythagoras theorem in 2D and 3D
  • Similarity, area and volume in similar figures
  • Congruent figures (SSS, SAS, ASA)
  • Chord angle and tangent properties of a circle
  • Properties of cyclic quadrilateral
  • Loci in two dimensions
  • Construction of bisection in angle and length

7. Mensuration

  • Length, area and volume
  • Mensuration of two dimensional shapes: rectangle, parallelogram, trapezium, triangle and circle
  • Mensuration of three dimensional: right circular cylinder, right circular cone, sphere, cuboid, pyramid and prism
  • Length of arc, areas of sector of a circle

8. Vectors and Transformation Geometry

  • Scalar and vector quantities
  • Understand and use of vector notation
  • Representation of vector by directed line segment
  • Parallel vectors, unit vectors and position vectors
  • Sum and difference of two vectors
  • Modulus of a vector
  • Multiplication of vector by a scalar
  • Find the resultant of two or more vectors
  • Transformation of plane
  • Combination of transformations
  • Multiplication of a vector by a matrix

9. Trigonometry

  • Use of sine, cosine and tangent of angles up to 180 degrees
  • Solution of problems in two and three dimensions by calculating and drawing
  • Angle of elevation and depression
  • Bearings

10. Statistics and Probability

  • Graphical representation of numerical data
  • Determination of mean, median and mode of discrete data
  • Calculation of estimate of mean of large number of quantities given in grouped frequencies
  • Determination of modal class and class containing median for grouped data
  • Understanding language and basic concepts of probability
  • Use of addition rule for two or more mutually exclusive events
  • Use of product rule for two or more independent events
  • Determination of probability of two or more independent events
  • Use simple conditional probability for combined events
  • Finding very simple conditional probability
  • Understanding and use the term 'expected probability'
